SILENCED NEVERMORE

The project Silenced Nevermore aims to address a modern world problem, the migrant crisis, and to resonate the stories of real people trying to escape a harsh reality.

With the poem "The Raven" by Edgar Allan Poe as a base, we created a juxtaposition between the mourning of the author and the desperation these migrants go through. In the Testimonies page, you can scroll through postcards with an illustration relative to a theme described in the poem. After an interaction with one of the postcards, you will be presented with a portion of a real testimony, a verse from the poem elusive to the theme of the testimony, a stamp of one of 18 mourning flowers and the recipient, who is always "To whom the angels name Lenore", since in the poem the author never names who he mourns, much as the unnamed people that lose their lives in the travessie.


Multimedia Object based in the poem “The Raven” by Edgar Allan Poe (1845), created and developed by Alexandra Mendes and Margarida Felício as an academic project within the curricular unit Project 3 - Multimedia Applications of the 2nd year of the Degree in Design and Multimedia in FCTUC - Faculty of Science and Technology of the University of Coimbra, under the orientation of the teachers Ana Boavida, Luís Pereira and Pedro Martins (January 2020).
